Kingsbridge

Kingsbridge is a market town in the South Hams district of Devon, England.
The town formed around a bridge which was built in or before the 10th century.
Kingsbridge is in fact a combination of two towns, Kingsbridge and Dodbrooke.
Dodbrooke was granted its own market in 1257 and had become a borough by 1319.
While Dodbrooke was originally considered to be the dominent of the two, Kingsbridge later expanded to include it.
The town center retains many 19th and 19th century buildings.
The shambles, or market arcade, was rebuilt in 1796 but retains its 16th century granite piers.
The former grammer school, now a museum, was founded and built by Thomas Crispin in 1670.
